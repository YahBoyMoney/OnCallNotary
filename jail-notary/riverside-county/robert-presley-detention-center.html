Robert Presley Detention Center Information

Robert Presley Detention Center (RPDC) is located in downtown Riverside and is the main jail facility for Riverside County.

Facility Location

Address: 4000 Orange Street, Riverside, CA 92501

Phone: 951-955-4500 (Main jail line)

RPDC is a high-capacity jail that houses pre-trial detainees for Riverside courts, as well as some sentenced inmates.

Visiting Procedures

Visits at Robert Presley Detention Center must be scheduled one day in advance by calling 951-341-8888 between 9:00 AM and 5:00 PM. Visiting days and hours depend on the inmate's housing location.

Currently, due to physical distancing requirements, only 2 visitors per inmate are allowed at a time. Visits are approximately 45 minutes each. Visiting slots fill up quickly at this busy downtown jail, so call as early as possible.

All adult visitors need valid photo ID. Visitors must pass through a security screening and must adhere to strict dress codes (no revealing or gang-related attire).

Note: No personal items (besides keys and ID) are allowed; lockers at the lobby might be available.

Attorney Visits: Attorneys can visit inmates at most hours but should coordinate with jail staff. For special visits (e.g., clergy or out-of-town immediate family in emergencies), contact RPDC administration for possible arrangements.

Inmate Locator & Mail

Use the Riverside County Inmate Locator online to find inmates in custody (Riverside Sheriff's "Inmate Information System"). You can search by name and find the inmate's location and booking number.

Mailing Address:

For Robert Presley, send inmate mail to: (Inmate Name & Booking Number), Robert Presley Detention Center, P.O. Box 710, Riverside, CA 92501.

All incoming mail is inspected; do not send cash or prohibited items. Acceptable mail includes letters, standard photographs (no Polaroids), and perhaps paperback books sent directly from a retailer (check jail rules).

Sending Money:

Funds for inmates at RPDC can be deposited via the jail cashier, mailed money orders (include booking number), or through online deposit services.

Our notary service can notarize any necessary forms like an Inmate Property Release form (if an inmate needs to authorize someone to pick up their property, which might require notarization of their signature).

Our Process at RPDC

The Robert Presley Detention Center has a visitor processing area where we will check in. As your notary, we register as a professional/legal visitor. We typically meet the inmate in a secure interview room, with a deputy present or observing through a window for security.

We present our ID and notary commission to jail staff if required. The inmate will be brought in with appropriate restraints as per jail protocol (usually they can still sign documents).

We then proceed with the notarization: verifying the inmate's identity (their jail wristband and booking photo, plus confirmation by the deputy, serve as ID), having the inmate sign and apply thumbprint (for journal and certain docs), and completing the notarial certificate.

We do this efficiently, usually within a 15-20 minute window, to respect the jail's schedule.

If you need a notary at the Robert Presley Detention Center, call or text (909) 751-8439. We often service RPDC multiple times a week and can accommodate last-minute requests whenever possible.

Documents We Notarize at Robert Presley Detention Center

Common documents we notarize for inmates at RPDC include:

Legal Documents for Court

Often inmates at RPDC have ongoing court cases. We notarize affidavits, declarations, or motions that an inmate needs to submit to the court. For example, a defendant's sworn statement or a notarized motion for release to a program.

Real Estate Transactions

If the inmate is involved in selling or refinancing a home, we can notarize deeds, escrow papers, or loan modification agreements at the jail. We coordinate with the escrow or title company to ensure all signatory pages and notary certificates are in order.

Parental Permission Forms

In downtown Riverside, we've helped notarize school forms for inmates who are parents – such as permission for a child's school field trip or enrollment documents that a jailed parent needs to sign and notarize.

Financial Powers of Attorney

Inmates use these to allow a family member to manage finances, access bank accounts, or handle tax returns while they are incarcerated. We bring the appropriate power of attorney form if needed and notarize the inmate's signature.

Business Contracts

Riverside is a hub of activity; if an inmate was a business owner or needs to sign off on a business agreement (like selling their business or transferring ownership shares), we can notarize those contracts so business operations can continue.

Property Release Forms

If an inmate needs to authorize the release of property held by the jail or police department, we can notarize the required authorization forms to help family members retrieve vehicles, personal belongings, or other assets.
