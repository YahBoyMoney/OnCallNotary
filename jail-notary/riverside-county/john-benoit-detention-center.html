John J. Benoit Detention Center Information

John J. Benoit Detention Center is a modern correctional facility located in Indio, serving the Coachella Valley and eastern Riverside County.

Facility Location

Address: 46-600 Oasis Street, Indio, CA 92201

Phone: 760-863-8888 (General facility line)

The John J. Benoit Detention Center, named after former Riverside County Supervisor John J. Benoit, is one of the newest detention facilities in Riverside County. This state-of-the-art correctional facility replaced the older Indio Jail and officially opened in 2019, significantly expanding the county's detention capacity in the eastern region.

Visiting Appointments

To visit an inmate at John J. Benoit Detention Center, you must schedule an appointment by calling 951-341-8888 (the central scheduling line for all Riverside County jails) between 9:00 AM and 5:00 PM, at least one day in advance.

This modern facility utilizes a video visitation system, allowing for more flexible scheduling options. Visiting hours vary by housing unit and are subject to change, so it's always best to confirm the schedule when booking your appointment.

Each visit typically lasts 30-45 minutes, depending on facility scheduling and inmate classification.

Visitor Rules:

All visitors must present valid government-issued photo identification. Visitors under 18 must be accompanied by a parent or legal guardian. The facility enforces a strict dress code that prohibits revealing clothing, gang-related attire, and clothing that resembles inmate uniforms.

Personal items, including cell phones and other electronic devices, are not permitted in the visiting area. Secure lockers are available for storing these items during your visit.

Inmate Search & Mail

Use the Riverside County online inmate locator to confirm if your loved one is at John J. Benoit Detention Center. When searching, the system will display "John J. Benoit Detention Center" or "Indio Jail" as the location.

Mailing Address:

Mail for inmates should be addressed as follows:

(Inmate Name & Booking Number)
John J. Benoit Detention Center
46-600 Oasis Street
Indio, CA 92201

Always include the inmate's full name and booking number to ensure proper delivery. All mail is inspected before being delivered to inmates, and certain restrictions apply to what can be sent. Photos, books, and magazines typically must come directly from publishers or approved vendors.

Money Deposits:

Funds can be added to an inmate's account through the lobby kiosk, online through the Riverside Sheriff's Department approved vendor, or by mailing a money order. For property release or money transfers requiring notarization, our mobile notary service can facilitate these processes on-site.

Documents We Notarize at John J. Benoit Detention Center

Common documents we notarize for inmates at John J. Benoit Detention Center include:

Powers of Attorney

General, limited, and durable powers of attorney allowing designated individuals to handle specific matters while the inmate is incarcerated. These documents are especially important for inmates facing longer sentences who need trusted representatives to manage their affairs.

Real Estate Documents

Property deeds, mortgage documents, refinancing agreements, and property transfers requiring notarized signatures. The Coachella Valley real estate market often necessitates timely document processing, and our service ensures these transactions aren't delayed due to incarceration.

Child Custody Documents

Temporary guardianship forms, custody agreements, and consent forms for minor children. These documents ensure children's needs can be legally addressed by designated caregivers during the parent's incarceration, providing stability for families during difficult times.

Business Documents

Business ownership transfers, corporate resolutions, partnership agreements, and other business-related documents. With the growing business community in the Coachella Valley, these documents often need timely processing to prevent disruption to ongoing business operations.

Financial Authorizations

Bank account access authorizations, financial power of attorney forms, and other documents allowing designated individuals to manage financial affairs. These ensure bills continue to be paid and financial obligations are met during incarceration.

Legal Affidavits

Sworn statements and affidavits for legal proceedings, including those related to the inmate's case or other civil matters. These documents often have strict requirements for notarization to be considered valid in court proceedings.
