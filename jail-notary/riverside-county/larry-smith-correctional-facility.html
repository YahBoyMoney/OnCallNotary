Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility Information

Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility is located in Banning, California, serving the Pass Area and eastern Riverside County.

Facility Location

Address: 1627 S. Hargrave Street, Banning, CA 92220

Phone: 951-922-7300 (General facility line)

The Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility, named after a former Riverside County Sheriff, is a modern medium-security facility serving the eastern part of Riverside County. It's one of the newer jail facilities in the Riverside County Sheriff's Department system.

Visiting Appointments

To visit an inmate at Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility, you must call 951-341-8888 (the central scheduling line for all Riverside County jails) between 9:00 AM and 5:00 PM to book a visit at least one day in advance.

Visiting hours vary by housing unit and change periodically, so it's always best to verify the current schedule when making your appointment. Each visit is typically around 45 minutes.

The facility offers video visitation, which means visitors and inmates communicate through a video screen rather than in person.

Visitor Rules:

All visitors must be 18 or older or accompanied by a parent/guardian. Valid government-issued photo ID is required. Dress code is strictly enforced (no revealing clothing, gang-related attire, or clothing resembling inmate uniforms).

Lockers are available for storing personal items during your visit, as cell phones and other electronic devices are not permitted in the visiting area.

Inmate Search & Mail

Use the Riverside County online inmate locator to confirm if your loved one is at Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility (search by name, and the system will show "Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility" as the location).

Mailing Address:

Mail for inmates should be addressed as follows:

(Inmate Name & Booking Number)
Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility
1627 S. Hargrave Street
Banning, CA 92220

Always include the inmate's booking number for faster processing. All mail is opened and inspected for contraband before delivery to inmates.

Money Deposits:

Funds can be added to an inmate's account through the lobby kiosk, online through the Riverside Sheriff's Department approved vendor, or by mailing a money order. For property release or money transfers requiring notarization, we can help facilitate these processes directly at the facility.

Our Approach at Larry D. Smith

When performing notary services at this facility, we check in as legal visitors (notary public) and are typically granted access to meet with the inmate in a designated area. The process may involve video communication systems depending on the inmate's housing unit.

We come fully prepared with all necessary notary supplies, including our seal, journal, and pens. Since reading glasses are often needed for document review, we recommend ensuring the inmate has access to their glasses if they require them.

After completing the notarization, we can either provide the documents to a designated family member who meets us at the facility or arrange for secure delivery to the required destination. This eliminates the need for multiple visits and ensures your documents are processed efficiently.

Documents We Notarize at Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility

Common documents we notarize for inmates at Larry D. Smith Correctional Facility include:

Powers of Attorney

Both general and limited powers of attorney allowing designated individuals to handle specific matters while the inmate is incarcerated. We ensure these documents meet all legal requirements to be effective for their intended purpose, whether for financial institutions, property management, or healthcare decisions.

Real Estate Documents

Deeds, mortgage documents, refinancing agreements, and property transfers that require the inmate's notarized signature. With property values in Riverside County continuing to rise, these documents often need timely processing to prevent financial complications for families.

Child Custody Documents

Temporary guardianship forms, custody agreements, and consent forms for minor children while a parent is incarcerated. These documents ensure children's needs can be legally addressed by designated caregivers during the incarceration period.

Business Documents

Business ownership transfers, corporate resolutions, partnership agreements, or other business-related documents requiring the inmate's notarized signature. Many inmates in this facility have ongoing business interests that require proper documentation to prevent disruption.

Vehicle Transfers

DMV forms for transferring vehicle ownership, power of attorney for vehicle-related matters, or other documents relating to the sale or management of vehicles owned by the inmate. These prevent vehicles from being impounded or creating financial burdens during incarceration.

Court Documents

Affidavits, declarations, and other court-related documents that require notarization for legal proceedings. These may be related to the inmate's case or to other civil matters requiring their sworn statement or signature.
