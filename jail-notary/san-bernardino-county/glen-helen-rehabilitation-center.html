Glen Helen Rehabilitation Center Information

Glen Helen Rehabilitation Center (GHRC) is a San Bernardino County jail facility located in Devore (north San Bernardino area).

Facility Location

Address: 18000 Institution Road, Devore, CA 92407

Phone: 909-708-8371 (Main number for Glen Helen and centralized inmate info line for SB County)

Glen Helen is spread over 9 acres and has multiple housing units for various security levels. It houses male and female inmates, typically those serving county sentences. Many inmates here are in work release programs or serving shorter sentences.

Visitation Details

Visiting at Glen Helen is by appointment and usually takes place Wednesday through Saturday, 9:00 AM to 7:00 PM. All visits must be scheduled via the Visiting Center at (909) 887-0364 at least one day before the visit.

Glen Helen's visiting schedule may allow for evening visits (till 7 PM) which is a bit later than some other facilities. Each inmate is typically allowed one visit per day, with a maximum of 2 adult visitors.

Because Glen Helen is in a more remote location, plan to arrive early to account for the drive and check-in process. As with all SB County jails, visitors must pass a security screening – no contraband, phones, or bags.

Lockers may be available to store your items or you should leave them in your vehicle.

Inmate Locator & Contact

Check if an inmate is at Glen Helen using the county's online inmate lookup. You'll need the inmate's name or 10-digit booking number. Since Glen Helen often houses sentenced inmates, they might also show up with a projected release date in the system.

For any questions, you can call Glen Helen's main line (909-708-8371) which connects to inmate information.

Mail:

Inmates at Glen Helen Rehabilitation Center can receive mail at P.O. Box 9490, Devore, CA 92407. Be sure to include the inmate's name, booking number, and housing unit if known. The facility name "Glen Helen Rehab Center" should be on the address as well.

Sending Money:

Glen Helen inmates have trust accounts; funds can be deposited online or via money order. There is also a kiosk at the facility for in-person deposits.

If you plan to bring a money order when coming for a notarization, we can guide you on how to fill it out for the inmate.

Documents We Notarize at Glen Helen Rehabilitation Center

Common documents we notarize for inmates at GHRC include:

Temporary Guardianship Agreements

Many Glen Helen inmates are serving short sentences; if a single parent is incarcerated, they may need to notarize a temporary guardianship form so a relative can legally care for their children during their incarceration.

Work Release or Program Documents

Inmates in work release or alternative sentencing programs might need to sign documents for employers or program administrators. We notarize any forms that require official certification, such as affidavits of residence or compliance.

Inmate Marriage License Applications

Glen Helen, being a "rehabilitation center," sometimes accommodates inmate marriages. The inmate will need to sign the marriage license application or a notarized affidavit for a marriage by proxy. We help couples move forward with their wedding plans.

Release of Information Forms

Inmates often need to authorize release of medical, financial, or legal information to their family or lawyer. We notarize forms like Authorization to Release Medical Records, so family can speak to doctors on the inmate's behalf.

Property Powers of Attorney

If the inmate left vehicles or property behind, they may need to notarize DMV power of attorney forms (to allow someone to sell a car or update registration) or real estate power of attorney (to let a spouse manage housing matters).

General Legal Documents

Any other legal documents requiring notarization, including court filings, settlement agreements, insurance forms, and other official paperwork that requires the inmate's notarized signature.
