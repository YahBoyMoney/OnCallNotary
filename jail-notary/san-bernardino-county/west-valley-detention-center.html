West Valley Detention Center Information

West Valley Detention Center (WVDC) is one of the largest jails in California, located in Rancho Cucamonga, San Bernardino County.

Facility Location

Address: 9500 Etiwanda Ave, Rancho Cucamonga, CA 91739

Phone: (909) 708-8371 (Main line for facility administration)

WVDC is a San Bernardino County Sheriff's facility with a capacity of over 3,300 inmates, handling a high volume of bookings and releases each year.

Visiting Information

Visits at West Valley Detention Center are by appointment only. Visiting days are typically Wednesday through Sunday, 8:30 AM to 9:00 PM. All visits must be scheduled at least a day in advance through the Visiting Appointment Center at (909) 887-0364.

Appointment hours are Tues–Sat, 8:00 AM – 5:30 PM to schedule a next-day visit. Each inmate is usually allowed a limited number of visits per week (e.g., two visits).

Visitors should check in early (30 minutes prior) and follow facility rules: valid ID required, dress code enforced, no cell phones or electronics, and no personal items beyond keys and ID are allowed in the visiting area.

For attorney or professional visits, West Valley allows access outside normal visiting hours; attorneys and bail bond agents may visit at any time, though bail agents must have a request and follow specific rules.

Inmate Locator & Facility Resources

To find an inmate at WVDC, use the San Bernardino County Sheriff's Inmate Locator online or call the inmate information line at (909) 708-8371.

The SB County Inmate Locator web tool lets you search by name or booking number and will provide the inmate's location and booking information.

Sending Mail:

Address mail to inmates at WVDC using their name and booking number, and the facility's mailing address: Inmate Name & Booking #, West Valley Detention Center, 9500 Etiwanda Ave., Rancho Cucamonga, CA 91739 (confirm if a P.O. Box is used by calling the jail).

All mail is inspected. You can also deposit money into an inmate's account via the jail's lobby kiosk or online deposit service.

Inmate Services: West Valley provides access to phone calls (through collect call or prepaid services) and commissary. We can help notarize any forms related to setting up inmate phone or commissary accounts if needed.

Documents We Notarize at West Valley Detention Center

Common documents we notarize for inmates at WVDC include:

Financial Power of Attorney

Enabling an inmate to authorize someone to handle banking or property transactions while they are incarcerated. This legal document transfers decision-making authority for financial matters to a trusted individual.

Real Estate Documents

Such as grant deeds, refinance documents, or sale papers if the inmate must sign to sell a property or refinance a mortgage. We can notarize deeds or escrow documents at the jail.

Parental Consent Forms

If the inmate is a parent who needs to notarize consent for a child's travel, school enrollment, or medical treatment by a guardian while they're incarcerated.

Affidavits and Sworn Statements

Any affidavit (e.g., a statement for use in court, or a declaration of paternity) that needs the inmate's sworn and notarized signature to be legally valid.

Business Contracts or Settlements

If the inmate is a party to a legal settlement, business contract, or needs to endorse insurance settlements, we can notarize those signatures on-site at West Valley Detention Center.

Property Transfer Documents

Documentation to transfer ownership of property, vehicles, or other assets that require notarized signatures to be legally binding during the inmate's incarceration.
