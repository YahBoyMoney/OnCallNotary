High Desert Detention Center Information

High Desert Detention Center (HDDC) is a San Bernardino County jail located in Adelanto, serving the High Desert region.

Facility Location

Address: 9438 Commerce Way, Adelanto, CA 92301

Phone: (760) 530-9300

HDDC is a newer facility (opened in 2006) with a capacity of over 2,100 inmates. It houses pre-trial detainees and sentenced inmates from the High Desert communities.

Visiting Info

Visiting days at High Desert Detention Center are generally similar to other SB County jails (Wednesday–Saturday, with possible Sunday or different schedules for different housing units). All visits must be scheduled by calling (909) 887-0364 in advance (the centralized scheduling line).

Visitors should verify HDDC's specific visiting hours, as they may have set time slots. On visiting day, arrive early for check-in and bring valid photo ID.

HDDC follows the same county-wide Visiting Guidelines: no cell phones or cameras, modest attire only, and only two people (or as limited by current rules) per visit due to space.

If you are coming to accompany our notary for the inmate notarization (for example, if you're an attorney or family member bringing the documents), please inform us so we can coordinate with jail staff.

Inmate Information

Use the San Bernardino County inmate locator to find if someone is at HDDC. HDDC, like other SB jails, also provides an inmate information line at 909-708-8371 for status and location inquiries.

Mail and Money:

To send mail to an inmate at High Desert, address it with the inmate's name and booking number. You may use the facility's street address (9438 Commerce Way) for mail, or a P.O. Box if one is designated.

Include a return address on the envelope. To send funds, you can drop off a money order at the jail or use the approved online deposit system (the county uses a vendor for electronic deposits; details are on the SB Sheriff's website).

We can notarize any Inmate Trust Account forms if an inmate needs to authorize someone to manage their account.

Documents We Notarize at High Desert Detention Center

Common documents we notarize for inmates at HDDC include:

Property Deeds & Transfer Documents

If the inmate has property in the High Desert area (or anywhere) that needs to be sold or transferred, we'll notarize grant deeds, quitclaim deeds, etc., right at HDDC.

Affidavit of Support or Parental Rights

In cases where an inmate must provide an affidavit (for example, to support a partner's immigration paperwork or to delegate parental decision-making), we handle those notarizations in custody.

Legal Settlements & Lawsuit Documents

Inmates involved in civil lawsuits (perhaps related to an accident or other civil matter) may need to sign settlements or agreements. We coordinate with attorneys to get those documents notarized inside HDDC.

Consent for Minor's Medical Treatment or Travel

If an inmate parent needs to sign a notarized consent for a child's surgery, or a letter permitting the child to travel with a guardian, we provide that service at High Desert Detention Center.

Power of Attorney Documents

Comprehensive power of attorney forms allowing inmates to designate a trusted individual to handle their personal, financial, or medical affairs while they are incarcerated.

Financial and Insurance Forms

Documents related to insurance claims, pension distributions, student loan paperwork (for younger inmates), and other financial matters requiring notarized signatures.
