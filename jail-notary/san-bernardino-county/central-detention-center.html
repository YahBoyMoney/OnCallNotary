Central Detention Center Information

The Central Detention Center (CDC) is located in downtown San Bernardino and serves as a primary intake and booking facility.

Facility Location

Address: 630 East Rialto Ave, San Bernardino, CA 92415

Phone: (909) 386-0969

The Central Detention Center is operated by the San Bernardino County Sheriff's Department and houses both county inmates and some federal inmates in custody.

Visiting Hours & Rules

Inmate visiting at Central Detention Center is by appointment. Visits are typically allowed Wednesday through Saturday, and must be scheduled one day in advance by calling the San Bernardino County centralized Visiting Center at (909) 887-0364.

Visitors should arrive 30 minutes early for check-in and adhere to the dress code and property rules (no cell phones, no revealing clothing, etc.).

Always check current visiting hours as they can change. All visitors must be on the approved visitor list and have valid ID. Minors must be accompanied by an adult, and former felons need prior permission to enter jail grounds.

For professional visits like notary services, we coordinate with jail staff in advance to ensure a smooth process.

Inmate Locator & Resources

To find out if your loved one is at CDC, use the San Bernardino County Inmate Locator tool online. You can search by name or booking number.

The county's inmate information page provides additional resources like how to post bail and contact inmate services. For general questions, you can also call the inmate information line at (909) 708-8371.

How to Send Mail:

Inmates at Central Detention Center can receive mail; all mail must include the inmate's full name and booking number. Mail should be addressed to the inmate at the jail's mailing address (check with the facility for the correct mailing address).

Do not send cash or prohibited items in mail. To send money for an inmate's commissary (trust account), you can use the jail's approved vendors or lobby kiosk.

Documents We Notarize at Central Detention Center

Common documents we notarize for inmates at San Bernardino Central Detention Center include:

Power of Attorney Forms

Enabling an inmate to designate a trusted person to handle finances or make decisions (property sales, child care, etc.) on their behalf while incarcerated.

Child Custody or Guardianship

Affidavits allowing an inmate to grant temporary custody of their child to a family member during their incarceration period.

Vehicle Release Forms

Notarizing authorization to release an impounded car or personal property that may have been seized during arrest.

Inmate Marriage Documents

Marriage license affidavits or consent forms for marriage by proxy, if the inmate is getting married while incarcerated.

Bail Bond Documents

Property bond agreements, promissory notes, or stipulations that require the inmate's notarized signature for bail proceedings.

Letters of Authorization

Any letter an inmate needs notarized to authorize someone to act on their behalf (e.g., authorization to retrieve personal records or handle DMV matters).
