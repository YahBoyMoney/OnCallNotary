Los Angeles County Jail Notary Services

We provide professional notary services at all Los Angeles County detention facilities, ensuring your documents are properly executed even when your loved one is incarcerated.

Los Angeles County operates the largest jail system in the United States, with multiple facilities throughout the county housing thousands of inmates. Each facility has specific protocols for notary services, and our experienced team is familiar with all of them.

Our mobile notary service travels to all Los Angeles County detention centers, providing professional notarization for various documents including powers of attorney, real estate documents, custody arrangements, and more. We understand the urgency often associated with these documents and strive to schedule appointments promptly.

All of our notaries are commissioned by the State of California and maintain thorough records of all notarizations in compliance with state regulations. We bring all necessary supplies, including our official seal, journal, and pens, ensuring a complete and legally valid notarization every time.

Men's Central Jail & Twin Towers

Located in downtown Los Angeles, these adjacent facilities form the core of LA County's jail system. Men's Central Jail (MCJ) is one of the largest jails in the world, while Twin Towers Correctional Facility houses both general population inmates and those requiring medical or mental health services.

Location

Men's Central Jail: 441 Bauchet Street, Los Angeles, CA 90012
Twin Towers: 450 Bauchet Street, Los Angeles, CA 90012

Visiting Information

Visits must be scheduled in advance through the LA County Sheriff's Department website or by calling (213) 473-6100. For notary services, we typically enter as legal visitors, which often requires advance scheduling. We're familiar with both facilities' protocols for notary visits.

Our Approach

Due to the size and complexity of these facilities, we recommend scheduling notary services at least 48 hours in advance when possible. We're experienced with the specific security procedures at these downtown facilities and can navigate them efficiently to ensure your documents are properly notarized.

Documents We Notarize at Los Angeles County Jails

Common documents we notarize for inmates at Los Angeles County detention facilities include:

Powers of Attorney

General, limited, and durable powers of attorney allowing designated individuals to handle specific matters while the inmate is incarcerated. These documents are especially important for inmates facing longer sentences who need trusted representatives to manage their affairs.

Real Estate Documents

Property deeds, mortgage documents, refinancing agreements, and property transfers requiring notarized signatures. With LA County's high property values, these documents often need timely processing to prevent financial complications for families.

Child Custody Documents

Temporary guardianship forms, custody agreements, and consent forms for minor children. These documents ensure children's needs can be legally addressed by designated caregivers during the parent's incarceration, providing stability during difficult times.

Business Documents

Business ownership transfers, corporate resolutions, partnership agreements, and other business-related documents. In LA's diverse business environment, these documents often need timely processing to prevent disruption to ongoing business operations.

Financial Authorizations

Bank account access authorizations, financial power of attorney forms, and other documents allowing designated individuals to manage financial affairs. These ensure bills continue to be paid and financial obligations are met during incarceration.

Legal Affidavits

Sworn statements and affidavits for legal proceedings, including those related to the inmate's case or other civil matters. These documents often have strict requirements for notarization to be considered valid in court proceedings.
