Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do I need to have ready for notarization?

You should have all documents that need to be notarized, as well as a valid government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport, etc.). Some documents may require additional witnesses or information.

How much does a mobile notary service cost?

Our basic notarization fee is $15 per signature (the maximum allowed by California law). Travel fees start at $35 depending on your location. Special services like jail visits or loan signings have package pricing.

How quickly can you arrive for an emergency notarization?

For emergency situations, we can often arrive within 1-2 hours in most parts of San Bernardino County. Please call us directly for the fastest service in urgent cases.

Do you provide notary services in jails and correctional facilities?

Yes, we specialize in jail notary services and are familiar with the procedures at all San Bernardino County detention facilities. We can help coordinate with facility staff to arrange the visit.

What forms of payment do you accept?

We accept cash, credit/debit cards, Venmo, Zelle, and PayPal. Payment is due at the time of service unless other arrangements have been made in advance.
